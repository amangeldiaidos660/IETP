The purpose of the grant and the relevance of the topic

The aim of the proposed project is to study the dielectric properties of single-component Dirac plasmas of different geometries and the stopping power of a planar plasma layer. The dynamic characteristics of the plasma will be numerically and graphically analyzed under large-scale changes in the parameters of charged particle systems. The concept of the project is to study electrodynamic characteristics of planar and bulk systems of charged particles, in particular, their dispersive properties and ability to absorb energy of moving test charges. To study the dynamic characteristics of high-temperature systems, random phase approximation is used, and in the case of low temperatures, the theoretical analysis developed by the members of the research team using the moment method is used. The obtained results can be used, in particular, to study the dynamical properties of a single-component plasma consisting of Dirac electrons in 2D and 3D geometries. Analysis of the stopping power of flat plasma sheets with respect to moving particles in them is also of undoubted interest for controlled fusion problems.

Results achieved

2021

The longitudinal dielectric function is found and the collective modes in a three-dimensional Dirac plasma are studied in the random phase approximation (RPA). The conditions for calculating the electrodynamic characteristics of Dirac 3D plasma are formulated. Characteristic features of Dirac systems of charged particles are shown - the presence of a characteristic length and an optical collective mode. The general expression of the dielectric function was obtained. The dispersion of waves in a one-component system of massless charged fermions is determined. The case of small wave numbers is considered. Normal and anomalous variance regions are found for different relationship parameters. Expressions of dielectric functions and dispersion relations for waves are obtained under various limiting conditions. Non-degenerate and degenerate plasma cases are considered. The dependence of wave propagation frequency and damping reduction on wave numbers is analyzed.
