Grant funding For scientific and (or) scientific and technical projects for 2021-2023 (36 months)

The name of the priority direction of science development: 1. Energy and engineering. The name of the specialized scientific direction: 1.1 Heat energy and the impact of the energy sector on the environment, energy saving. Grant code and title: AP09259554 Development of criteria for assessing the effect of geomagnetically induced currents on main power lines and oil and gas pipelines.

The purpose of the grant and the relevance of the topic The purpose of the project is to develop criteria for evaluating negative electromagnetic effects and to create a device for recording telluric currents, taking into account space factors, during the operation of terrestrial systems (power lines, pipelines) in the territory of Kazakhstan. Brief description of the project idea - the values of geomagnetically induced currents (GIC) are determined based on the measurement data of parameters of the geomagnetic field and telluric currents, as well as numerical calculations using models of ionospheric currents and earth conductivity. Criteria for the identification and assessment of adverse electromagnetic effects on main power transmission lines and oil and gas pipelines will be developed on the territory of Kazakhstan. This project is aimed at solving the issues of technosphere safety (including environmental aspects) of safe operation of main power lines and increasing the durability of oil and gas pipelines during their use, taking into account space factors (geoeffective solar phenomena, accelerated drift). magnetic pole). The relevance of the tasks set by the project is determined by the fact that the terrestrial technological infrastructures, including, in particular, the power lines, are affected by the ATS, regardless of their location. In countries with large territories, such as Kazakhstan, long main power lines and pipelines contribute to the increase in the values of induced currents during extreme geoeffective solar events.

AP09261161 3D modeling of turbulent heat and mass transfer processes in physically and chemically active media
Fundamental and applied research in physics and astronomy

Ғname of the priority direction of science development: scientific research in the field of natural sciences. Name of the specialized scientific direction: Fundamental and applied research in the field of physics and astronomy, applied research. Code and name of the grant: AP09261161 "3D modeling of turbulent heat and mass transfer processes in physically and chemically active media".

The purpose of the grant and the relevance of the topic: The purpose of the project: research and development of turbulent heat and mass exchange processes and the formation of harmful substances during combustion of solid fuel in the combustion chambers of real energy objects, using modern methods of physical, mathematical and 3D computer modeling. recommendations for practical application of the obtained results. Currently, there is increasing interest in the study of heat and mass transfer processes in reacting media, as the results of such studies have wide practical applications. The study of such environments is relevant due to the need to develop processes of "clean combustion" of fuel while maintaining strict standards of emission of harmful substances into the atmosphere. Particular attention is paid to the creation of new computational models that allow the most accurate description of the physico-chemical processes occurring during fuel combustion in real geometric areas (combustion chambers of energy objects). The problems of creating effective computational algorithms for the study of turbulent heat and mass transfer processes in physically and chemically active environments are particularly relevant and require deep analysis from a fundamental and applied point of view.

The name of the priority direction of science development: scientific researches in the field of natural sciences. Name of the specialized scientific direction: Fundamental and applied research in the field of physics and astronomy. Grant code and title: AP09259876 "Physics of Small Stellar Objects"

The aim of the project is to study the matter evolution of compact stellar objects such as neutron stars, white dwarfs, pleurions and post-stellar nebulae. In particular, modeling of processes that cause rapid cooling of compact stars and high-energy radiation with them, and calculation of reaction cascades with nuclei and nuclear clusters in extreme conditions. One of the priority areas of research in the field of nuclear physics and nuclear astrophysics is the study of compact stellar objects such as white dwarfs, neutron stars, pleurions, as well as post-stellar nebulae. Such star formations are common in the Universe and are of particular importance for the study of thermonuclear processes and the evolution of stellar matter in a wide range of astrophysical phenomena. The theoretical analysis of the elemental composition of these stars, the study of the mechanisms of emission and ejection of matter from these objects show the abundance of some chemical elements in the Universe, such as isotopes of lithium, Na22 and Al26. In addition, the high-energy radiation from these objects is able to travel enormous distances and reach the Earth, which makes it possible to study the properties of interstellar matter. Of particular interest is the study of the behavior of matter under extreme conditions and at densities close to the nucleus.
