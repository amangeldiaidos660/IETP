AP08857288 Use of modern energy-saving technologies in industrial boilers and development of mechanisms for environmentally friendly burning of powdered coal
Environmental impact of the heat-power and energy sector, energy saving

The name of the priority direction of science development: Energy and mechanical engineering. The name of the specialized scientific direction: Heat and energy and the impact of the energy sector on the environment, energy saving, applied research. Code and name of the grant AR08857288 "Application of modern energy-saving technologies in industrial boilers and development of mechanisms for environmentally friendly burning of powdered coal."

The purpose of the grant and the relevance of the topic The goal of the project is to optimize combustion processes of coal with high ash content, to reduce harmful dust and gas emissions (CO and CO2 carbon oxides, nitrogen oxides NOx, sulfur oxides SO2, ash, etc.) CHP PK-39 boiler) use. The relevance of the project lies in obtaining updated methods of burning low-grade Kazakh coal, which allows to significantly improve the environmental and economic indicators of the operating boiler units, taking the PK-39 boiler at the Aksu CHP as an example. The use of energy-efficient technologies as a means of optimizing the processes of burning solid fuel allows Kazakhstan's energy companies to realize economical production of electricity and thermal energy, to fulfill important environmental tasks, thereby improving the energy and environmental situation in the republic. country.

Fundamental and Applied Research in Physics and Astronomy. Grant code and title AP08857359 Measurement of neutron fluxes from the WWR-K reactor with large area neutron detectors for environmental impact assessment.

The purpose of the grant and the relevance of the topic The goal of the project is to develop a methodology for determining the neutron flux density from the WWR-K reactor, based on the consideration of neutron flux attenuation by various materials, and to create an automatic system that includes a complex of equipment to ensure neutron flux attenuation. continuous automatic, remote and express determination of neutron flux from a nuclear reactor in real time. Human industrial activity (including nuclear and thermal energy) has a significant man-made impact on the natural environment. In this regard, it is very relevant to study the impact of neutron flow from reactors on the environment. Since the VVR-K research reactor is located near the city of Almaty, the assessment of the impact of neutron fluxes on the environment becomes a very urgent issue. In the framework of this project, the interaction of neutrons with matter was studied, and they were studied in polyethylene, graphite, lead, beryllium, etc. It is recommended to determine the characteristics of absorption and reflection in different media. The project proposes to compare the intensity of neutron fluxes recorded by SNM-15, SNM-18 and helium-2 proportional counters when they pass through different media. It is planned to create an economical and ergonomic device for recording neutrons. The experiment will be conducted at the Institute of Nuclear Physics with a stable neutron background at the WWR-K reactor. Counters of this type are successfully used for recording neutrons. Recording of thermal neutrons will be carried out by nuclear reaction of 10B(n,α)7Li in BF3 gas enriched with 10B isotope filled with counters. The length of the counter is 2 m, the diameter is 15 cm.
